excerpt: "I used human B cells as a model to investigate a key question in developmental biology: during cell reprogramming, how do cells integrate extrinsic signals with their intrinsic cellular state? \n <br/><img src='/images/bcell_schematic.png' alt='B Cell Schematic' style='display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; width: 75%'> Using single-cell genomics, I was able to quantify the relative contribution of intrinsic cellular status during a 12 day B cell reprogramming experiment. Intrinsic effects were strong, clones often adopted the same fates, revealing that adding extrinsic factors like cytokines to cells is only part of the equation for reprogramming outcomes. These findings have important implications for long-standing goals of reprogramming or controlling cells for cell-based therapies and tissue engineering. You can find the full paper [here](https://www.life-science-alliance.org/content/lsa/6/3/e202201792.full.pdf). \n\n <br/><img src='/images/lineage_tracing_measurement.png' alt='Lineage Graph' style='display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; width: 65%'>"
